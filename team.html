Who We Are

Meet the faces behind UChicago FEMMES.

Emilee Urbanek

Program Director:
Curriculum and Web

Emilee ('16) is a Computer Science major passionate about science and music. She hopes to inspire girls in the Chicago area to get excited about computer science and its application to other STEM fields just as her parents and teachers have inspired her. When she can, she enjoys snowboarding.

Panya Gupta

Program Director:
Capstone and Logistics

Panya (’17) is a Computer Science major and aspiring Religious Studies minor, passionate about teaching, modern art, science, and drumming. She is excited for the opportunity to share her love for STEM with other women through working with FEMMES and teaching in middle and elementary school math classes. She enjoys traveling and learning new languages.

Amanda Aizuss

Assistant Program Director:
Curriculum and Web

Amanda ('17) is a Computer Science major originally from Los Angeles. Passionate about technology for as long as she can remember, she occasionally makes YouTube videos where she discusses her favorite Mac & iOS Apps, tips, and consumer technology. She's excited to be part of FEMMES to help expose girls to the power and creativity that computer science offers.

Megan Renshaw

Logistical Head

Megan ('16) is a Computer Science and Mathematics major double major. She is the Logistics Head for FEMMES and one of the cofounding members. In her spare time, Megan enjoys playing the ukulele and guitar.





Rachel Pinsker

Curriculum Head

Rachel ('16) is majoring in Computer Science at UChicago. She worked on iOS while interning at Facebook the past two summers. Rachel is currently the Head of Curriculum for FEMMES and is really excited to get middle school girls excited in Computer Science and other STEM fields!

Richard Hanson

Webmaster

Richard ('16) is a Linguistics turned Computer Science major at UChicago. He is passionate about committees and music and pursues both outside the classroom. He is grateful for the opportunity to try and be part of the solution to some of the gender problems in tech.

Sydney Ko

Graphics Head

Sydney ('18) is a Computer Science and Visual Arts double major. Originally from San Jose, she lived abroad in Seoul, Korea for over a decade. She became involved in the tech gender parity movement while working for Girls Who Code last summer. When she's not coding, Sydney is probably cheering at a football game or ice skating on the midway.

Jenee Fowler

Creative Head

Jenee spends her time coming up with new ways to approach problems and inspire those around her to step outside of their comfort levels. With extensive background in volunteering for organizations like hospice and home health as well as political campaigns, Jenee loves to be involved in her community and always seeks ways to improve the world around her.

Jenna Clemens

Communications Director

Jenna ('16) is a Computer Science major at UChicago. As a member of the STEM community, she hopes to be able to inspire more young women to learn about STEM opportunities and to find ways to make coding a more attainable skill. You can most likely find her (badly) dancing or eating her way through Chicago when she is not doing her homework or reading about women in STEM.

Bobby Kanuri

University Liason

Bobby (SM '17) is pursuing a graduate degree in Computer Science at UChicago. He is a veteran software developer who has worked with companies ranging from tiny tech startups to one of the worlds preeminent investment banks. He is grateful to the teachers who introduced him to programming at a young age and hopes to pay it forward by helping students of all ages and feels honored to work with FEMMES. In his free time he practices, professionally on occasion, photography.

Nasr Maswood

Community Liason

Sylvia Lobo

Treasurer

Sylvia ('17) is a Biology and Psychology double major, as well as a premed. She loves FEMMES because it promotes the sciences to young girls. Her hobbies include bike riding and petting cute animals. She is excited to see FEMMES grow!

Jasmine Mithani

Curriculum Writer

Jasmine Mithani '17 is majoring in Interdisciplinary Studies in the Humanities with a focus on interactive media and gender. She currently interns as game developer at DoSomething.org, making games to promote positive offline impact. She is also one of the editors of Sliced Bread, the on-campus literary magazine, is the chair of the university ACM-W chapter, and is one of the organizers of the UChicago Game Design group. Her interests lie at the crossroads of social activism and emerging technology, and she is especially dedicated to advocacy for STEM education, gender equality, and consent. She writes poetry, plays obscure art games, and occasionally sleeps.
